# Pure Sine Wave UPS  

This was my **final year Bachelor’s project**, completed in 2009 during Pakistan’s energy crisis, when frequent load-shedding created a strong demand for reliable backup power systems.  

## Background  

At that time, most locally available UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supplies):  
- Did **not provide a true sine wave output**, leading to poor compatibility with sensitive electronics.  
- Relied on **bulky transformers**, which made them inefficient and large in size.  

Our project aimed to design a **proof-of-concept UPS** that:  
- Generated a **pure sine wave output**.  
- Reduced the **size and weight** by eliminating the bulky transformer.  

The prototype was later taken up by **Prodigy Technologies, Pakistan**, who mass-produced the concept commercially.  

## My Contribution  

I served as the **technical lead** for this project, with the following key responsibilities:  

- **PCB Design**  
  - Designed a compact **single-layer PCB** due to budget and manufacturing constraints.  
  - Optimized component placement and routing to ensure minimal noise and maximum efficiency.  

- **Firmware Development**  
  - Programmed a **PIC16 microcontroller** in **Assembly language**.  
  - Developed the algorithm for **sine wave generation** using **PWM (Pulse Width Modulation)**.  
  - Implemented waveform shaping and filtering to achieve a smooth and reliable sine output.  

- **System Integration**  
  - Coordinated the hardware and firmware development with the rest of the team.  
  - Verified inverter efficiency and waveform quality against reference standards.  

## Outcome  

- Successfully demonstrated a **compact, transformer-less, pure sine wave UPS**.  
- Validated as a **proof-of-concept** and later adopted for **mass production by Prodigy Technologies**.  
- Showcased early expertise in **PCB design, and low-level microcontroller programming**.  

## Technologies & Tools  

- **PIC16 Microcontroller** (Assembly language)  
- **PWM-based sine wave generation**  
- **OrCAD for PCB design** (single-layer optimization)
