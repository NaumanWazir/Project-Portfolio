# Multiple Sensor Integration for Fairing Separation

## Overview
Fairing separation is a critical stage in **satellite launch vehicles**, typically executed using **pyrotechnic charges**.  
To ensure reliability and safety, the mechanical team needed accurate measurements of the **trajectory of separated fairing parts** in order to determine the optimal charge size.  

## My Role
I was tasked with designing and implementing a **multi-sensor measurement system** to capture the motion and behavior of fairing sections during separation tests.  

## Technical Details
- **Sensors Integrated**:
  - Multiple **digital gyroscopes** for angular velocity measurement.  
  - **Accelerometers** for linear acceleration tracking.  
  - **Proximity sensors** for separation distance monitoring.  
- **Data Acquisition**:
  - Synchronized data collection with accurate **timestamping**.  
  - Configurable communication setup:
    - **Wired interface** for controlled lab testing.  
    - **Wireless data transfer** via **XBee modules** for field testing.  
- **Post-Processing**:
  - Collected data was processed to **reconstruct the trajectory of separated parts**.  
  - Trajectory plots enabled the mechanical team to fine-tune the amount of pyrotechnic charges required.  

## Outcome
- Successfully provided **reliable sensor data** for fairing separation tests.  
- Helped the mechanical design team **optimize pyrotechnic charge requirements**, ensuring safe and effective satellite deployment.
